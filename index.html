WHY THIS EXISTS

Web3 has no memory of who's actually reliable

Wallets are pseudonymous by design — which is great for privacy, but it means every new interaction starts from zero trust. A builder who's shipped five successful projects looks identical, on-chain, to a wallet created five minutes ago. That gap is exactly what enables scams, ghosted bounties, and hiring based on vibes instead of evidence.

Today, without this

Trust lives off-chain, or nowhere

  • Reputation is scattered across GitHub, Twitter, and Discord DMs
  • Anyone can claim credentials with no way to verify them on-chain
  • New wallets and proven builders look exactly the same
  • Fake attestations are as easy to fake as a five-star review
With GIWA Builder Rep

Reputation that's earned, weighted, and portable

  • Every attestation is on-chain, timestamped, and permanent
  • Vouches are weighted by the issuer's own earned reputation
  • Your track record travels with your wallet — not locked to one app
  • Sybil attacks are expensive: fake accounts start at weight zero

How the weight is calculated

Every attestation carries a weight based on the issuer's own reputation at the moment it's issued. New accounts start small — but every builder can grow their influence by earning attestations themselves.
